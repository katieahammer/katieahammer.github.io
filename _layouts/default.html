Summer STEM Research 2024

University College Dublin - Professor Brett Becker

Independent research project conducted at University College Dublin during the summer of 2024 under the guidance of Professor Brett Becker. This research explored the boundaries of ChatGPT in scientific inquiry, investigating whether the AI could make scientific discoveries autonomously. The project delved into various aspects including anthropomorphism, error messaging, and Conway's Game of Life, with the primary goal of determining if ChatGPT could independently generate scientific insights and discoveries through strategic prompting techniques.

It's Dangerous to Prompt Alone! Exploring How Fine-Tuning GPT-4o Affects Novices' Programming Error Resolution

Published by IEEE

This collaborative research paper, co-authored with another student and a graduate student, investigates whether ChatGPT could create better error messages for Python code. The study compared different approaches including one-shot prompting, few-shot prompting, and fine-tuning techniques to evaluate their effectiveness in generating improved error messages that would be more helpful for programmers debugging their code. Published in IEEE.
